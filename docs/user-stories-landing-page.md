# toddleSpot — Landing Page User Stories

> Goal: Build a pre-launch landing page to collect waitlist emails and validate demand.

---

## Brand Direction

- **Palette:** Soft pastels — pink, yellow, light blue — with black outlines
- **Style:** Hand-drawn doodle illustrations, playful hand-lettered headings
- **Texture:** Speckled/paper background (not sterile white)
- **Tagline:** "Baby friendly venues at your finger tips"
- **Tone:** Warm, approachable, parent-to-parent

---

## User Stories

### Must Have

#### 1. Understand the value proposition
> **As a** parent visiting the site,
> **I want to** immediately understand what toddleSpot does,
> **So that** I can decide if it's relevant to me.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- Hero section with clear headline and tagline
- Explains the core problem (finding baby-friendly venues is hard)
- Explains the solution (crowdsourced baby-friendly ratings from real parents)
- Communicates this within 5 seconds of landing on the page

---

#### 2. Sign up for launch updates
> **As an** interested parent,
> **I want to** enter my email to join a waitlist,
> **So that** I get notified when the app launches.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- Email input field with clear CTA button (e.g. "Join the waitlist")
- Email validation (valid format required)
- Confirmation message after signup (e.g. "You're on the list!")
- Emails stored securely (GDPR-compliant)
- CTA visible without scrolling (above the fold) and repeated at the bottom

---

#### 3. See what the app will offer
> **As a** visitor,
> **I want to** see a preview of key features,
> **So that** I understand what to expect from the app.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- Feature section showing 3-4 key capabilities:
  - Search for baby-friendly venues nearby
  - See real ratings from other parents (changing facilities, high chairs, pram access, etc.)
  - Leave your own reviews
  - Map view to find venues near you
- Uses icons or illustrations consistent with brand style

---

#### 4. Feel trust and credibility
> **As a** parent,
> **I want to** know this is built by someone who understands my problem,
> **So that** I feel confident signing up.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- Brief "About" or "Our story" section
- Mentions it's built by a parent (Aaron) from personal experience
- Authentic tone — not corporate

---

### Nice to Have

#### 5. Share with other parents
> **As a** visitor who's interested,
> **I want to** easily share toddleSpot with friends,
> **So that** other parents in my area can sign up too.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- Share buttons (WhatsApp, copy link — these are the channels parents use)
- Or a simple "Tell a friend" CTA

---

#### 6. See the launch area
> **As a** visitor,
> **I want to** know where toddleSpot is launching,
> **So that** I know if it's relevant to my location.

**Acceptance criteria:**
- Mention of Upminster / Essex as the launch area
- Framing that suggests expansion is planned ("Starting in Upminster, growing from there")

---

## Page Structure (suggested)

1. **Hero** — Headline, tagline, email signup CTA, brand illustration
2. **Problem** — "Finding baby-friendly places shouldn't be this hard"
3. **Features** — 3-4 key features with icons
4. **About** — Built by a parent, for parents
5. **Launch area** — Starting in Upminster
6. **Final CTA** — Repeated email signup
7. **Footer** — Links, socials, legal
